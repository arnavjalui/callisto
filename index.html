Reasons to use Virtual Reality

  1. The fast rate of growth of the VR Industry and the ever decreasing costs of a VR Headset make the Indian Education System a perfect beneficiary from the current VR Industry outburst.

  2. VR based learning will keep school-going children attached to the education system and will reduce dropout rates.

  3. VR Headsets bought in bulk quantities can be procured for a cheaper cost and can reach out to government-run schools alongwith the obvious addition to primary schools due to their inexpensive nature.

  4. The high and easy availability of smartphones helps in the cause of propagating an effective education system through the use of virtual reality.

  5. Many internet giants like Google are experimenting by introducing the VR system in classrooms and the results on the learning habits and understanding capabilities of the students are nothing short of being very impressive.

Advantages of VR in Education

  1. One Size Doesn't Fit All.

    One of the important things to remember with virtual reality is that, although this technology may be more expensive than traditional eLearning, there are plenty of different options to suit the budget of organizations who are determined to embrace virtual reality. The Google Cardboard headset costs $15 each, whilst the more advanced headsets such as Oculus Rift are on the market for around $500.

  2. Immersive Learning.

    In the industry we constantly strive to create an engaging and exciting learning journey for employees. The nature of virtual reality means that this experience is probably one of the best examples of creating a fun and effective learning environment, which has shaken up the Learning and Development industry. In addition, research shows most of the population learn best kinaesthetically, meaning virtual reality is a great option for this type of learner.

  3. We All Make Mistakes.

    One of the charming advantages of using virtual reality technology is that end-users can learn without worrying about making mistakes. Too often when completing eLearning in the workplace, employees can be conscious and pressurized to ensure they pass courses first-time. However, completing learning through a virtual reality headset creates a separate virtual entity for users, creating a safe environment where they can comfortably learn at their own pace.

Drawbacks of VR in Education

  1. Integration Issues.

    Currently, virtual reality technology seems to be a disconnected solution as it's likely to be too costly to implement all educational learning into a 3D experience. Therefore, careful consideration would need to be taken to integrate virtual reality into the other learning that the organization offers - whether it's eLearning on computers and mobile devices or offline training such as classroom activities. This is not impossible, but strategic thought is required to ensure the excitement of virtual reality doesn't detract from the importance of other learning options.

  2. High Initial Investment.

    Despite there being a variety of headsets and phones to meet different budgets, there's no denying that investing in virtual reality technology is going to be a costly choice; particularly schools and organizations with large numbers of students. This is likely to mean that organizations will decide on investing in small numbers of devices to share amongst students. Although the high availability of smartphones in the current household scenario means that cost can be lowered by deploying personal mobile devices or tablets, which will help lower the cost of initial investment.

  3. Breathing Space.

    It's safe to say that we won't want to complete our virtual reality learning at your desk for the risk of bumping into colleagues and falling over chairs. So teachers and other responsible officers will need to find a suitable space on the school premises to allow users to have personal space to comfortably complete learning. This moves Learning and Development back towards traditional classroom training which eLearning was designed to move away from in the first place and restricts the freedom of the learner.

Expeditions AR - Bringing the world into the classroom

Applications of Virtual Reality

  1. In medical studies, to enable students to know the human body structure.

  2. In scientific research laboratories, so that scientist can easily research on a specific topic.

  3. In entertaiment like in games and movies, to make the gaming experience more real and to allow individual to experience adventures under extreme conditions.

  4. In driving schools, as it give a real look of roads and traffic.

  5. In military training, for the soldiers to get familiar with different areas in the battlefield.

Final Words

The fact that The New York Times recently supplied more than one million subscribers with Google Cardboard headsets to access its newly launched VR experiences has further advanced accessibility and mainstreaming of the device, as well as this innovative means of media consumption. Educators and students alike are seeking an ever-expanding immersive landscape, where students engage with teachers and each other in transformative experiences through a wide spectrum of interactive resources. In this educational reality, VR has a definitive place of value.
